Ramen in Salmon Broth

my go to cold days dinner recipe

November 1, 2015 Jane Doe

Recipe

For the Broth:
Salmon Head 1 (can be substituted with salmon bones or tails)
Ginger 4 Slices
Lemongrass 1 Stalk
Boiling Water 500ml (enough to cover the ingredients)
Light Coconut Milk 200ml
Fish Sauce 1 Tbs
Mirin 1 Tbs
Salt, to taste

For the Toppings: Chinese Cabbage 1/4 , Roughly Chopped
Enoki Mushrooms 200g
Ramen Noodles 200g

For the Salmon Head:
Mayonnaise 10g
White Sesame Seeds 1 Tsp

To Finish:
Coriander 10g, Finely Chopped
Lime Juice 1/2

method

Preheat a stainless steel saucier or pot over medium-high heat. Once hot, add 1 tablespoon of oil and carefully place the salmon head in the pot. Sear each side for approximately 3 minutes, until golden brown.

Add the ginger slices and chopped lemongrass stalk to the pot. Stir-fry for about 1 minute, until fragrant.

Pour in the boiling water and bring the mixture to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat, cover the pot, and simmer for 20 minutes. Skim off any impurities that rise to the surface during the simmering process.

After simmering, remove the salmon head, ginger, and lemongrass from the broth. Transfer the salmon head to an air fryer-safe container and discard the rest. Drizzle with mayonnaise and sprinkle with sesame seeds.

Air fry the salmon head at 180Â°C for 10 minutes, or until crispy and golden.

Add the light coconut milk, fish sauce, mirin, and salt. Bring the broth back to a boil.

Add the ramen noodles, mushrooms, and shredded cabbage to the broth. Cook for 5 minutes, or until the noodles are tender and the vegetables are cooked through.

Ladle the ramen broth, noodles, and vegetables into bowls. Garnish with fresh coriander and a squeeze of lime juice. Serve the crispy salmon head on the side
