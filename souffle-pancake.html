Japanese Souffle Pancake

my go to guilt-free dessert

November 1, 2015 Jane Doe

Servings

Per Serving: 3 Pancakes
No. of Serving: 2

Recipe

Medium Eggs 3
Caster Sugar 35g
Milk 45ml
AP Flour 38g
Baking Powder 2g
Vanilla Extract 1/8 Tsp

Toppings:
Salted Butter 5g
Maple Syrup 1-2 Tsp
Icing Sugar 1 Tsp (Optional)

method

Separate the egg yolks from the egg whites. Beat the egg whites until they reach the "foamy stage." Gradually add sugar in three batches, ensuring each addition is fully incorporated before adding the next. Continue beating until the egg whites become shinier and smoother, stopping when they reach soft peaks.

In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks and vanilla extract until they turn pale yellow. Add the milk and stir until combined. Sift in the flour and baking powder, and gently mix into the egg yolk mixture using a "dragging motion." This helps prevent over mixing the flour, which can cause the pancakes to be rubbery.

Carefully add 1/3 of the beaten egg whites to the egg yolk mixture, folding gently until just combined. Then, transfer the mixture to the remaining egg whites and gently fold until fully combined.

Transfer the pancake batter into a piping bag (optional, alternatively, you can scoop the mixture with a ladle.)

Preheat a stainless steel frying pan over medium-low heat until it reaches 115-120Â°C. Spread butter on the pan and use a kitchen paper towel to evenly coat the surface, removing any excess grease.

Pipe or scoop the pancake batter onto the pan, leaving at least 1 cm between each pancake as they will expand while cooking. Splash a few drops of water into the pan and cover with a lid to steam-fry the pancakes. Cook for 5 minutes.

Flip the pancakes over and splash a few more drops of water into the pan. Cover again and steam-fry for another 5 minutes.

Transfer the pancakes to a plate. Add a slice of cold salted butter on top and drizzle with maple syrup. Optionally, sprinkle with icing sugar for extra sweetness. Serve immediately and enjoy the light, fluffy texture of your souffle pancakes!
